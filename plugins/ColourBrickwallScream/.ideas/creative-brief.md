# Creative Brief: ColourBrickwallScream

## Hook
*"Paint the wall red. Then hit it."*
A harmonic saturator and brickwall limiter that doesn't just tame peaks — it deforms them with colour, character, and attitude. ColourBrickwallScream is a tone-shaping weapon for producers who want destruction with personality.

## Description
ColourBrickwallScream is a two-stage processor: a harmonic saturation engine (the "Scream") followed by a hard brickwall limiter (the "Wall"). Between them sits a tone-shaping and colouring stage that lets users sculpt the character of the distortion before it hits the ceiling.

The plugin is built around the idea of **temper variants** — distinct saturation/clipping personalities that range from warm tube-like softness to aggressive digital hardclip brutality. Each variant changes the harmonic profile of the saturation curve, giving the plugin radically different sonic identities within a single interface.

### Signal Flow
```
Input → Drive/Scream Stage → Tone Shaping → Colour Stage → Brickwall Limiter → Output
```

### Key Behaviours
- **Drive/Scream:** Pre-gain into the saturation stage. Low settings add warmth; high settings push into full harmonic mayhem.
- **Character (Temper Variants):** Selects the saturation curve type. Options span from Tube, Tape, Transformer, Diode, Bitcrush, to Full Scream. Each has a distinct harmonic signature.
- **Tone:** Post-saturation tilt EQ or presence boost/cut — shapes the brightness or darkness of the distorted signal.
- **Colour:** Adds harmonic colouration independent of drive — think of it as "what flavour is the dirt?" Blends in mid-range resonance, warmth, or air depending on the selected Character.
- **Attack Character:** Controls how the brickwall limiter responds to transients. Fast = punchy and aggressive, Slow = smeared and crushed. Affects the feel of the wall, not just the ceiling.

## Aesthetic Direction
Industrial. Aggressive. Alive.

Think cracked concrete walls with glowing heat elements. Dark background with high-contrast orange/red/white accents. Meters that look like they're straining. Knobs with mechanical precision but visual wear. The plugin should look like it belongs in a factory that makes music that hurts.

## Target Use Cases
- Drum bus / master bus limiting with character
- Vocal saturation and tone sculpting
- Aggressive sound design (synths, guitars, full mixes)
- Loudness maximisation with harmonic personality
